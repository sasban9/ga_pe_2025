Definition

What is a Small
Language Model?

  • Typically under 7 billion parameters (vs. 70B–1T+ for LLMs)
  • Designed for specific domains — not general-purpose chat
  • Runs on edge hardware, laptops, phones, and on-prem servers
  • Fine-tuned for precision: higher accuracy on narrow tasks
  • Examples: Phi-3 Mini, Mistral 7B, Gemma 2B, LLaMA 3.2 1B

Playbook

How to Adopt SLMs
in Your Org

  • 1. Identify high-frequency, narrow tasks — document classification, Q&A, extraction
  • 2. Select a base model — match params to hardware constraints
  • 3. Fine-tune on your domain data — even 1,000–5,000 examples is enough
  • 4. Quantize for production — INT4/INT8 cuts memory by 4–8×
  • 5. Deploy with RAG — pair with your knowledge base for living accuracy
  • 6. Monitor & iterate — SLMs are cheap enough to retrain frequently

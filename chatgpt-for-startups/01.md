## **Module 1 — ChatGPT as Your Startup Copilot**

**Duration:** ~10 minutes
**Goal:** Understand how to make ChatGPT act like a dedicated teammate who already knows your startup — instead of treating it like a one-off tool.

---

### **1. Opening Hook (Voiceover ≈ 1 min)**

> “Imagine hiring a team member who instantly learns your entire business model, remembers your tone, and works 24/7 — writing, coding, and brainstorming on demand.
> That’s what ChatGPT becomes when you train it as your *Startup Copilot*.”

**Visual:** A founder surrounded by chat bubbles labeled “Marketing,” “Tech,” “Finance,” “HR,” with ChatGPT in the center acting as the connector.

---

### **2. Why Context Is Everything (Voiceover ≈ 2 min)**

Explain that ChatGPT’s power scales with *context.*
Most people use it as a blank chat box — but startups can feed it their mission, tone, audience, and workflows so it replies *as if it were part of the company.*

**Example:**
A founder types:

> “We build eco-friendly home cleaning products for urban millennials.”

If that’s baked into ChatGPT’s memory or system prompt, it will generate on-brand marketing lines, not generic ones.

**Demo:**
Show two responses to the same question “Write a tagline for our product.”
One without context → “Clean your home with nature’s touch.”
One with context → “Sustainably spotless — eco-smart cleaning for modern city living.”

**Visual:** Split-screen comparison.

---

### **3. Teaching ChatGPT About Your Startup (Voiceover ≈ 3 min)**

Guide learners through a quick, hands-on setup.

**Step 1 — Gather Your Startup DNA**
Ask them to note down five things:

1. Startup name
2. One-line mission
3. Target customers
4. Product or service summary
5. Preferred tone (funny, expert, casual, bold, etc.)

**Step 2 — The “Startup Copilot Prompt” Template**

```
You are the AI Copilot for a startup called [Startup Name].  
Our mission is [Mission].  
We serve [Customer Type] by [Product Summary].  
Our tone is [Tone Style].  
When answering, always align with our brand voice and keep replies short, practical, and startup-friendly.  
First, confirm you understand our identity, then wait for my next task.
```

**Voiceover Tip:**

> “This single prompt transforms ChatGPT from a generalist into your personal AI teammate.”

Encourage learners to paste this into ChatGPT and see it respond with acknowledgment like,

> “Got it. I’m now your AI Copilot for GreenNest — eco-friendly cleaning for urban millennials.”

**Visual:** Screenshot of ChatGPT reply confirming company understanding.

---

### **4. Adding Memory & Tools (Voiceover ≈ 2 min)**

Explain that ChatGPT can remember details (via custom instructions) and integrate with tools like Notion, Slack, or Zapier for deeper workflow automation.

**Demonstrate conceptually:**

> “When you say *‘Summarize this week’s sales chat from Slack’*, ChatGPT can pull structured insights if connected through automation tools.”

Encourage learners to explore integrations later — but emphasize today’s focus is *teaching ChatGPT context.*

---

### **5. Mini Exercise (Voiceover ≈ 1 min)**

Learners write their Startup Copilot Prompt, paste it into ChatGPT, and then ask:

> “Generate 3 tagline options and 2 tweet ideas for our startup.”

Then they compare the quality with a prompt given *before* training ChatGPT.
They’ll see instantly how context upgrades results.

---

### **6. Wrap-Up & Takeaway (Voiceover ≈ 1 min)**

> “From now on, never start a chat cold. Begin by briefing ChatGPT like a new hire.
> In later modules, we’ll plug this Copilot into marketing, product, and operations — but this setup is your foundation.”

**Slide Summary:**
• Context > Commands
• Teach before you ask
• Save your Copilot Prompt for all future sessions

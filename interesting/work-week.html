“The Changing Work Week: Rethinking Time, Productivity & Life”

A short course that explores how the traditional 7-day work/rest rhythm is evolving in the modern world, especially in the age of remote work, AI, and globalization.

📘 Mini Course:
The Changing Work Week

Sunday is no more a Sunday. Monday is no more a Monday.

🔹 Module 1:

The Traditional Work Week – A Legacy of the Industrial Era

Lecture 1: Origins of the 5-Day Work Week

The religious and industrial roots of Sunday rest and Monday grind.
Henry Ford and the 40-hour workweek.

Lecture 2: The Meaning of “Weekend”

How cultures shaped rest days differently: from Western Sundays to Middle Eastern Fridays.
Reflection Prompt:
What does “weekend” mean to you personally — a time for rest, catch-up, guilt, escape, or something else?
🔹 Module 2:

The New Normal – Shifting Work Rhythms in the 21st Century

Lecture 1: Remote Work & the Death of the Calendar

Work from home, asynchronous schedules, timezone-agnostic teams.
How freelancers, creators, and digital nomads defy 9-to-5.

Lecture 2: AI, Automation & Always-On Expectations

ChatGPT never sleeps. Should you?
The pressure of instant replies and “always available” mentality. Exercise: Track your energy, work, and rest patterns for one week — compare to a traditional calendar.
🔹 Module 3:

Consequences – The Psychological & Social Impact

Lecture 1: Time Disorientation & Burnout

When all days feel the same — cognitive fatigue and blurred boundaries.

Lecture 2: When Families Don't Share Time Off

The new “asynchronous households” — partners and friends off on different days. Discussion Prompt: What social rituals are we losing? What might replace them?
🔹 Module 4:

Alternatives - Inventing New Rhythms

Lecture 1: 4-Day Work Weeks, Flexi-Time, and Micro-Retirements

Experiments around the world: Iceland, Japan, UK.
What companies gain when employees work less.

Lecture 2: Your Personal Week Design

Designing a week that works for your body, brain, and life goals. Project: Redesign your ideal 7-day cycle. Name the days. Define the purpose of each.
🔹 Module 5:

The Philosophy of Time – What Really Matters

Lecture 1: Beyond Productivity – Time as Meaning

Why we fear Mondays. Why we romanticize Sundays.
The existential cost of clock-based living.
Final Reflection: If there were no fixed calendar or clock, how would you measure a successful life?

🧾 Deliverables:

  • Weekly time map
  • Reimagined 7-day cycle
  • Journal reflections
  • A manifesto: “My New Week”

Section 1

🧭 The Pattern of Internet Gold Rushes

Every gold rush follows the same 4-stage pattern.

Recognizing it early means you can ride the wave instead of chasing it.

The 4 Phases:

1. **Skepticism:** “Who’ll use the internet?” “Apps are toys.” “AI will never replace workflows.” 2. **Early Adoption:** Builders explore and experiment. The public laughs. The pioneers quietly earn millions.
Mass Adoption:The market floods; visibility becomes expensive; competition rises.
Commoditization: Only those who built early ecosystems keep reaping rewards.
Analogy

Just like miners who didn’t just find gold — but sold shovels, water, and land rights — in digital gold rushes, the real winners are those who create the *platform tools*, not just use them.

Key takeaway line: “Don’t be the last person mining. Be the first person *owning the mine.*”
🧩 Section 2:

From Tools to Teammates

Concept: AgentKit marks the moment AI stops being a *tool* and starts being a *teammate*.
Explanation: * Pre-2025, you used tools like ChatGPT manually — prompt in, result out. * Post-AgentKit, you *deploy* an autonomous agent that can act, plan, remember, and connect to other tools. * These aren’t chatbots. They’re digital workers who never sleep.
Real-life analogy:s “In 1995, you built a website to show your business online. In 2008, you built an app to interact with users. In 2025, you’ll build an agent to *work for you.*”
**Visuals:** Show side-by-side clips — person prompting ChatGPT vs. an agent autonomously booking flights, sending emails, and updating a CRM.
### 💰 **Section 3: The New Frontier — The AgentKit Store (4 mins)** **Concept:** The AgentKit Store is the *App Store moment* for AI.
**Explanation:** * OpenAI’s AgentKit lets anyone build, test, and publish autonomous agents. * These agents can connect to real-world APIs — meaning they can *do things*, not just *say things*. * The store will soon be flooded with everything from “travel planners” to “sales closers.”
**Example (analogous to early App Store):** * Early app gold rush: Instagram (photo sharing), WhatsApp (messaging), Uber (transport). * AgentKit equivalents could be:
* **“StoryCrafter”** — an agent that writes and publishes stories across platforms. * **“BizLaunch AI”** — an agent that creates, registers, and markets a new brand for you. * **“TaskHawk”** — an agent that runs your email, schedule, and errands 24/7.
**Key takeaway line:** “The world is about to be filled with digital workers. The question is: will you build them, own them, or compete with them?”
### 🧠 **Section 4: Think Like a Prospector (3 mins)** **Concept:** You don’t need to know how to code — you just need to think like a digital prospector: spot value before others see it.
**Framework for Thinking:** * **Observe pain:** What do people waste time or money on daily? * **Imagine automation:** Could an agent handle 80% of it autonomously? * **Map value:** If it saves time or earns money, it’s gold.
**Exercise:** Ask learners to quickly list 3 daily annoyances in their life or work. Prompt: “If an agent could do that for you, what would it look like?” Encourage 30-second brainstorms, even simple ones like “a WhatsApp reply manager” or “daily content curator.”
🚀 Section 5: Why Timing is Everything Concept: Timing, not talent, makes millionaires in digital revolutions.
Explanation: * The first 10,000 apps on the App Store made more money than the next million combined. * Early YouTubers built media empires when ad rates were high and competition was low. * Right now, AgentKit agents have near-zero competition and massive discoverability.
Key takeaway line “Every day you wait, another prospector claims a piece of land. You don’t need to mine every mountain — just stake your first claim.”

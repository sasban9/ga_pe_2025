Here are twelve **task-based demos** you can show to intermediate users of ChatGPT (i.e., people who have been using ChatGPT since 2023) to highlight the *new features added in 2025*. For each task I provide a description, why it matters, and sample user prompt + expected outcome. You can choose to build a short workshop or ‘hands-on lab’ around these.

---

### 1. Build a Custom GPT (“Your Personal AI Assistant”)

**Why it matters:** In 2025 you can create and deploy specialised GPTs (i.e., custom versions of ChatGPT) tailored to your role or task. ([Medium][1])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Create a custom GPT for a product-manager role: it should ask about feature specs, competitor features, suggest roadmap items, and output a prioritisation table.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT walks you through building (or scaffolding) that custom GPT, sets up its behaviour, maybe suggests trigger questions and ends up with the “GPT” you can interact with as if it were an assistant.

---

### 2. Leverage Memory that Actually Persists

**Why it matters:** ChatGPT in 2025 improves how it retains and uses longer-term memory of your preferences, project context, writing style, etc. ([Medium][1])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Last week you learnt that I prefer concise executive-level summaries with bullet-style. For this week’s market-update report generate a summary of X with those preferences remembered.”
> **Expected outcome:** The assistant recalls your previous preference (concise, bullet-style) without you having to re-state it, and tailors the output accordingly.

---

### 3. Use Voice Conversations in Real Time

**Why it matters:** Discussing with ChatGPT is no longer just typing; you can talk to it, get real-time voice responses. ([Medium][1])
**Demo prompt:**

> (Speaking into mic) “Walk me through the main differences between AWS and Azure for serverless in plain language.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT responds via voice, with natural cadence, maybe clarification questions, giving you an interactive voice-based session.

---

### 4. Use File Uploads + Data-Analysis Mode

**Why it matters:** Upload your spreadsheets/CSVs/JSONs and ask the system to analyse them, visualise, summarise. ([Medium][1])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Here is a CSV of our last quarter’s sales by region. Upload it. Then please give me a visual breakdown of revenue by region, trend lines month-to-month, and call-out any outlier months.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT ingests the file, does data cleaning/analysis, produces charts, commentary, and tables.

---

### 5. Use “Apps in ChatGPT” via the Apps SDK

**Why it matters:** The 2025 release enables “apps” inside ChatGPT – interactive mini-tools you can call. ([OpenAI][2])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Search for and launch the ‘Budget Planner’ app inside ChatGPT. Then using that app, build a 12-month budget for a startup with $500k funding, allocating to marketing 40 %, R&D 30 %, operations 20 %.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT suggests the app, you open it, interact with it inside the chat interface, and get the budget output from the app logic.

---

### 6. Try the Latest Model: GPT-5

**Why it matters:** In 2025 the model behind ChatGPT has been upgraded (e.g., GPT‑5) with improved instruction-following, lower hallucination, faster responses. ([OpenAI][3])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Using the newest model, please summarise this dense research paper (I’ll paste it) in under 250 words, list three actionable insights for a tech startup and critique any assumptions you spot.”
> **Expected outcome:** A high-quality summary, precise actionable insights, and a critical evaluation — showcasing the upgraded reasoning quality.

---

### 7. Shopping & Web-Search Enhancements

**Why it matters:** ChatGPT’s web search and shopping features have been upgraded in 2025 so that you can ask for product recommendations with images, reviews, direct links. ([Reuters][4])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Help me pick a high-end 16 GB RAM 1 TB SSD laptop under ₹1,50,000 in India. Show 3 best options with pros/cons, reviews summary, and links to buy.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT uses live web data (or simulated) to fetch current models, shows comparison table, review summary, and purchase links.

---

### 8. Scheduled Tasks / Reminders (Agent-style Features)

**Why it matters:** ChatGPT now supports “Tasks” like reminders, scheduled future actions, behaving more like an assistant than just a chat. ([The Verge][5])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Set a weekly recurring reminder: every Monday at 9 am IST, ask me ‘What are your top three priorities this week?’. Also, please summarise last week’s actions before asking.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT confirms it’s set the task, and when the time arrives would remind you (or simulate this) plus produce a summary of last week’s actions.

---

### 9. Enhanced Voice Mode with Emotion, Intonation

**Why it matters:** The voice mode was improved in 2025 to sound more natural, expressive (empathy, sarcasm, natural pauses). ([The Times of India][6])
**Demo prompt:**

> (Voice) “Explain to me the difference between UX-design and UI-design, but do it as if you’re giving advice to a friend who is overwhelmed and worried.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT speaks back with appropriate empathy, slower pacing, natural tone, maybe a friendly remark, making the experience feel more human-like.

---

### 10. Region-Specific Subscription Plan: ChatGPT Go

**Why it matters:** In India (and potentially other markets) a new subscription tier ChatGPT Go was introduced in 2025, offering higher limits, more features at a lower cost. ([OpenAI Help Center][7])
**Demo prompt:**

> “What advantages do I get if I upgrade to ChatGPT Go in India versus staying on the free plan? Show a comparison chart and how I can upgrade.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT summarises the difference: higher message limits, larger file uploads, access to advanced data-analysis etc., and provides steps to upgrade in India.

---

### 11. Enhanced Safety/Parental Controls for Younger Users

**Why it matters:** As ChatGPT expands usage, new safety controls (especially for teens) were introduced in 2025 (monitoring, usage limits, content filters). ([WIRED][8])
**Demo prompt:**

> “I’m setting up a ChatGPT account for my 15-year-old. What parental-control features are available now (2025) and how do I enable them?”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT explains the new safety settings: content filters, usage time-blocks, memory disabling, voice/image generation toggle, how to opt-in, etc.

---

### 12. Long-Context + Better Reasoning / Code-Support Upgrades

**Why it matters:** The models in 2025 can handle longer context windows, improved reasoning, better code generation and interpretation. (see multiple sources about improved models) ([webasha.com][9])
**Demo prompt:**

> “Here is a 10,000-word technical spec document (I’ll paste it). Please review it, produce a list of five potential design flaws, suggest improvements, and generate a refactored pseudocode structure for the system.”
> **Expected outcome:** ChatGPT ingests the long spec (or simulates as if it did), identifies design flaws, suggestions for improvement, and outputs structured pseudocode — demonstrating long-context and advanced reasoning.

---

---

**How you might structure the demo session:**
Introduce the participants (already familiar with older ChatGPT) to the fact that many of these features are *new* in 2025. Then run through tasks 1-4 as interactive “your turn” exercises. Follow with 5-8 as group explorations of advanced use cases (apps, shopping, voice). Then tasks 9-12 as deeper features showing model upgrades, subscription/plan differences, safety ecosystem. At the end invite users to brainstorm “what would I build now that I couldn’t before”.

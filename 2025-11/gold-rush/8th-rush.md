Y2K remediation (late 1990s). Companies paid heavily for programmers to fix date bugs. Low complexity, high demand, short window.

Dot-com build-out (1997–2001). Everyone needed a website. Money flowed into any internet idea. Infrastructure and hosting companies gained most.

Search engine SEO and AdWords arbitrage (2003–2010). Early users exploited cheap keywords to drive traffic and resell leads at high margin.

Web 2.0 social platform tools (2005–2012). Facebook app ecosystem, Twitter tools, blog CMS plugins. Small utilities at scale made revenue.

Mobile app boom (2009–2016). App Store and Play Store early adopters earned from simple apps. Distribution was frictionless, competition initially low.

Cloud migration (2012–2018). Companies moved workloads to AWS/Azure. Consultants and SaaS monitoring tools grew rapidly.

Crypto/Blockchain wave (2016–2021). Exchanges, wallets, mining, NFTs. Notable for fast speculation cycles and extreme volatility.

Each followed the same pattern:
New platform appears → distribution friction decreases → first builders who ship simple products early capture outsized returns.

Lesson 3.1: Explaining Medical Information in Simple Language

Why This Matters

* Many patients have low health literacy (average reading level is around 6th - 8th grade in most countries).
* Confusion about medical instructions often leads to non-adherence, readmissions, or unsafe behavior.
* Nurses can use AI to simplify, personalize, and culturally adapt information quickly, saving time while ensuring clarity.

Core Learning Points

1. Levels of Explanation
* Professional Level: For doctors and clinical staff (uses medical terms).
* Plain-Language Adult Level: For the patient.
* Child-Friendly Level: For kids or caregivers who need super-simple terms.
Example with Hypertension:

Professional:

“Patient presents with elevated systolic blood pressure >140 mmHg, risk of left ventricular hypertrophy if untreated.”

Plain-language adult:

“Your blood pressure is higher than normal. If not treated, it can strain your heart.”

Child-friendly:

“The blood is pushing too hard on the walls of your pipes (blood vessels), and we need medicine to help it flow smoothly.”
2. How AI Helps
* AI can instantly rephrase doctor’s notes into different levels of complexity.
* It can also adjust tone: calming, encouraging, authoritative, or explanatory.
* Best practice: Always review and edit AI text before sharing with patients.
3. Tools You Can Use
* **ChatGPT (with medical prompt templates)**
* **Glass AI** (medical summarization)
* **Mediscribe AI** (structured notes → simplified summaries)
* **Healthily** (ready-made condition explanations)

Practical Workflow Example

1. Nurse receives discharge summary: “Patient to continue amlodipine 5mg OD. Monitor for ankle edema. Recheck BP after 2 weeks.”
2. Use AI prompt: “Rewrite this note for a patient with no medical background. Keep it short, clear, and reassuring.”
3. AI Output: “Take your blood pressure medicine (amlodipine) once a day. Sometimes it may cause your ankles to swell. Please come back in 2 weeks to check your blood pressure again.”
4. Nurse reviews → adds empathy: “Please remember to take your medicine once a day, every day. If you notice your feet or ankles swelling, don’t worry, but let us know. We’ll check your blood pressure again in 2 weeks to make sure you’re doing well.”

Hands-On Activities

Rewrite Exercise

Take one real discharge summary (with identifying info removed) and generate 2 simplified versions:

* Plain adult language
* Child-friendly version

Tone Practice

Use AI to generate the same explanation in 3 tones:
* Reassuring
* Motivational (“You can manage this”)
* Neutral / informative

Roleplay

Nurse uses the AI output, then practices explaining it aloud with warmth and eye contact.

Quick Prompt Templates for Nurses

“Explain this medical note in plain language that a 12-year-old can understand.”
“Turn this discharge summary into simple instructions for the patient to follow at home.”
“Rewrite this in under 100 words, in a reassuring and friendly tone, without medical jargon.”
“Translate this explanation into Bengali/Hindi, keeping it simple and clear.”

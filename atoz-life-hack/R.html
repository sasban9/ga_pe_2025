Step R: Reputation Capital

Your Name is Your Currency

Key Idea: Reputation is a form of wealth.

It compounds. It pays you forever. And once lost, it is nearly impossible to rebuild.
There’s a story from the early days of Silicon Valley: A young founder had a habit of giving unrealistic time promises to investors and partners. He was talented, charming, and brilliant. But he delivered late. Every time. Eventually, people stopped taking his calls.
Meanwhile, another founder, with fewer skills and less genius, had a rule: “If I say I’ll do it, I’ll do it. No exceptions.” Five years later, everyone wanted to work with the second founder.
Not because he was the smartest.

But because his word meant something.

Talent gets attention.
Reputation builds empires.
When you were in school, your value was measured through:
* Marks * Grades * Certificates * Rankings
In the working world, the real currency is your reputation * What people say about you when you’re not in the room
* How reliable you are
* How you handle pressure
* Whether you follow through
Your reputation answers questions like: * Can people trust you to deliver? * Do you finish what you start? * Do you say what you mean and mean what you say? * Do you create value or drain it?

Reputation
=
Character + Consistency + Competence

You build it in three places:

1. **In your work** Show excellence even in small things. 2. **In your relationships** Leave people better than you found them. 3. **In your word** If you commit, you execute.

The Silent Rule of High Performers

**Overdeliver quietly. Underpromise intentionally. Never break your word.** This is what makes athletes, elite entrepreneurs, creators, and respected leaders magnetic.
## **REAL WORLD EXAMPLES** • Cristiano Ronaldo has teammates who say: “His work ethic speaks louder than his talent.” His reputation is built in training, not in trophies.
• Virat Kohli built his name not just through runs, but through consistency and relentless standards. • Apple’s brand reputation for simplicity is why customers line up before release day.
Reputation compounds. Like interest. Like momentum. Like gravity.

REPUTATION KILLERS (avoid these)

* Making promises casually * Delaying or disappearing * Doing the bare minimum * Blaming others * Being hard to rely on Each one slowly pulls you back into the **“average mindset.”**

REPUTATION BUILDERS (do these daily)

* Be on time * Do what you said you would do * Own mistakes without excuses * Finish what you start * Speak less, execute more This alone makes you **rare** in a world of talkers.

PRACTICAL ACTION EXERCISES

Exercise 1 — The 3 Commitments Audit

Write down: 1. One promise you made to yourself that you haven’t kept 2. One promise you made to someone else that you haven’t completed
3. One project you left unfinished **Your task:** Finish at least **one** of the above **today**.

Exercise 2 — The “Reputation Rule”

For the next 30 days:
If you say you will do something,
you must do it.
No exceptions. No excuses. No renegotiation.

You will feel your self-respect grow.

In Closing

Your reputation is not built in big heroic moments. It is built in the quiet, repeatable, invisible choices:
When no one is watching.
When it’s not exciting.
When it’s easier to skip.

Every time you follow through, you rewrite your identity: “I am a person who does what I say.”

People trust those people. Opportunities find those people. Success gravitates to those people. Protect your name.
Because your name will carry your life.

Every important decision gets lost in endless Slack threads. Meeting outcomes vanish. Compliance requirements get buried. Knowledge workers waste hours scrolling through conversations trying to find what matters. SlackToDoc automatically transforms your messy Slack discussions into structured Notion documentation in real-time. It identifies decisions, action items, and key insights, then organizes them into searchable, compliant docs that actually make sense.
The tool works invisibly - just tag @SlackToDoc in important channels or threads. It extracts the signal from the noise, formats everything cleanly, and syncs it to your team's knowledge base. No more copy-pasting or manual summarizing. Teams see productivity jumps of ~30% as critical information becomes instantly accessible instead of scattered across channels.
For companies with compliance needs (healthcare, finance, legal), SlackToDoc adds automatic policy flagging and audit trails. You charge $29/user/month for teams and $99/user/month for enterprises with custom compliance requirements. The expansion path is clear: start with Slack-to-Notion, then add Microsoft Teams, Discord, and custom knowledge bases. Target mid-sized companies (50-500 employees) drowning in digital conversations but lacking dedicated knowledge managers.
The initial product connects to Slack's API to capture conversations, uses sophisticated context models to identify important content, and pipes structured data to Notion via their API. You can prototype this with 5-10 customers manually before building the full automation. Early adopters become evangelists because the pain is universal - no one wants to lose critical decisions to the Slack void. Documentation shouldn't require extra work; SlackToDoc makes knowledge management automatic.
